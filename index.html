I am an Associate Professor in the Information School, Adjunct Associate Professor in Computer Science & Engineering. I lead an interdisciplinary group in Responsible Data Science with emphasis on urban applications. I was the Founding Associate Director in the UW eScience Institute; I continue to serve on the steering committee as a Senior Data Science Fellow. I am a co-founder of Urban@UW, and I lead UW's participation in the MetroLab Network and the Cascadia Urban Analytics Cooperative. I created a first MOOC on Data Science through Coursera, and I led the creation of the UW Data Science Masters Degree, serving as its inaugural Program Director and Faculty Chair. I serve on the Steering Committee of the Center for Statistics in the Social Sciences.

My group's research aims to make the techniques and technologies of data science dramatically more accessible and reliable, particularly at scale. Our methods are rooted in database models and languages, though we sometimes work in machine learning, visualization, HCI, and high-performance computing. We are an applied, systems-oriented group, frequently sourcing projects through collaborations in the physical, life, and social sciences.
